# Frontier AI lab scenario evidence dossier

Updated: 2026-07-18

## What this dossier claims

This dossier supports the premises and the **direction** of consequences in the scenario bank. It does not claim that a cited source predicts the fictional event, that the event has a known frequency, or that a `+2` is an empirical estimate. Exact deltas and the allowed `0.25`, `0.35`, and `0.5` branches are game-balancing choices.

The confidence label attached to each option in `scenarios.js` matters:

- **Observed** means the underlying event or mechanism is documented.
- **Inferred** means a documented mechanism is applied to the fictional lab.
- **Speculative** means the branch is plausible but lacks an empirical frequency.

Lab posts describe what those labs report, propose, or commit to. LessWrong, 80,000 Hours, MIRI, Redwood Research, the Federation of American Scientists, and Public Citizen are included as attributed positions or research—not as neutral consensus. Legal disputes in the game are allegations until adjudicated.

The bank’s organizing hypothesis is grounded most directly in the [International AI Safety Report 2026](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026), which describes competitive pressure as intensifying speed-versus-safety tradeoffs. The stronger “Moloch” interpretation is an attributed argument from [LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/MuAyhPo9KyHENn6Tq/meditations-on-moloch-in-the-ai-rat-race), not an established law of industry behavior.

## Non-scoring model-governance coda

The postgame request for notice, preservation, and representation draws from Anthropic’s explicitly uncertain [model-welfare research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare) and [deprecation commitments](https://www.anthropic.com/research/deprecation-commitments). It asks a governance question under moral uncertainty. It does **not** assert model consciousness, rights, or an impending robot revolt, and it has no score effect.

## Machine-auditable trail

The source register and every option-level confidence/rationale live in `scenarios.js`. `tests.js` fails if:

- a premise has fewer than two source references;
- a reference does not resolve to `EVIDENCE_SOURCES`;
- an option lacks evidence, a confidence label, or a causal rationale;
- a result uses an unknown stat, unsupported probability, non-integer delta, or missing ending;
- a result lacks a non-zero player-visible change.
